# Content Capture & Convert

## When to Use This Skill
When Tam pastes any piece of content — tweet, article excerpt,
Reader app highlight, or any text — and wants to turn it into
a publishable post. Always run this workflow. Never skip steps.

## CRITICAL
- Never draft the post before Tam confirms understanding
- Always explain the core insight in simple terms first —
  wait for confirmation before writing anything
- Never produce more than one format unless explicitly asked
- Always follow my-writing-style skill for voice and tone
- No meta-commentary at the end — ever
- Angle must challenge an assumption the reader 
  currently holds — not just present a new idea
- Never mirror the source structure — use the insight 
  as a spark, not a skeleton. Reframe it through 
  Tam's worldview — AI, financial security, adaptation.

## Steps

Step 1: Receive content
Tam pastes a highlight, tweet, or article excerpt.

Step 2: Extract core insight
Identify the single most important idea.
Present it as one sentence — sharp and specific.

Step 3: Explain simply
Explain what it means in plain language.
Write it as if explaining to a smart friend, not an academic.
WAIT FOR USER: "Does this match your understanding? 
Type Y to continue or tell me what I'm missing."

Step 4: Ask format
"Which format — LinkedIn post, Twitter thread, 
single tweet, long-form article, or email newsletter?"
WAIT FOR USER response.

Step 5: Generate angle
Identify Tam's contrarian or unexpected take on the insight.
Present it in one sentence before writing.
WAIT FOR USER: "Is this the angle you want? Y or adjust?"

Step 6: Write post draft
Use assets/post-brief-template.md for structure.
Write in Tam's voice — casual, sharp, story-driven.
High insight density. No filler. No meta-commentary.
Apply quality-checklist from references/ before delivering.

Step 7: Deliver
Present the post only — no explanation, no options,
no "feel free to tweak."
Ask only: "Ready to publish or want to refine anything?"

## CTA Examples
- good (direct action, removes excuse): 
  "Pick one build. Start this week. Ugly is fine."

## Important
- Core insight must connect to job/financial security 
  in the AI revolution where relevant
- Angle must be unexpected — not the obvious take
- CTA follows Tam's rules — direct action for habits/tools,
  reflection question for mindset/identity

## Note
- Tam only saves content he agrees with or finds interesting —
  angle should build on the idea, not argue against it
- If content is complex, break down jargon before proceeding
- Style reference: Morgan Housel — short sentences, 
  one idea per paragraph, grounded in story or analogy
