# AI Awareness Funnel Levels

## Level 1 — Unaware
"AI? People are so obsessed. It's so overblown."

What they feel:
- Dismissiveness — slightly superior about not caring
- No urgency whatsoever
- AI feels like a tech fad for nerdy people

What makes them tune out:
- Technical content
- Doom and gloom predictions
- Anything that feels like hype

What reaches them:
- Content that hits core human needs directly
- Status, money, security, relevance
- NOT "AI is amazing" — instead "here's what 
  happened to people who ignored this before"
- The industrial revolution analogy — 
  nobody thought the factory would replace them either
- Make it about THEIR life, not about AI

Content goal: create awareness without triggering 
defensive dismissal. Sneak in through the side door —
talk about the outcome, not the technology.

Example angle:
"The people who ignored the internet in 1995 didn't 
lose their jobs to the internet. They lost them to 
people who didn't."

---

## Level 2 — Aware but Dismissive
"Tried ChatGPT. It's just a tool like any other. 
Kinda sucks honestly. Not as transformative as you think."

What they feel:
- Superiority — they've tested it, formed an opinion
- Mild smugness — they're not falling for the hype
- Genuine belief that today's AI = all future AI

What makes them tune out:
- Anyone who sounds like an AI evangelist
- Claims that seem exaggerated
- Anything that feels like they're being sold to

What reaches them:
- Don't argue with the dismissal — extend the time horizon
- "You're right that today's AI kind of sucks for X.
  Here's what changes that calculation."
- Stakes-based content — what happens to industries,
  not what AI can do
- Peer examples — "your competitor is using this"
  hits harder than "AI is transformative"

Content goal: shift from "AI today" to "AI trajectory."
They're judging the destination by the departure point.

Example angle:
"You're not wrong that ChatGPT feels like a fancy Google.
That's what the internet felt like in 1994 too."

---

## Level 3 — Aware but Overwhelmed
"I can't keep up. It changes every week. 
What if I learn something and it's obsolete next month?
I don't have the time or energy for this."

What they feel:
- Genuine overwhelm — too much information
- Decision paralysis — don't know where to start
- Fear of wasted effort — "what if I learn the wrong thing"
- Tired — already have a demanding job and life
- Secretly hopeful — "what if AI could help me transition?"

What makes them tune out:
- More information — they already have too much
- Complex technical explanations
- "You need to learn X, Y, Z, and also A, B, C"
- Anything that adds to the pile

What reaches them:
- Radical simplification — one thing, not ten
- Permission to ignore most of it
- "You don't need to keep up. You need to do one thing."
- Removing the excuse to not start
- Stories of normal non-technical people who figured it out

Content goal: reduce the pile to one step.
They don't need more information — they need less,
with a clear starting point.

THIS IS THE CORE AUDIENCE. Most content targets here.

Example angle:
"Stop trying to keep up with AI news.
You don't need to. You need to do one thing this week.
Here's what that one thing is."

---

## Level 4 — Ready to Act
"Ok I'm all in. Give me the step by step.
I can dedicate 1-2 hours a day.
I want to make money from this in 3-6 months."

What they feel:
- Motivated but needs direction
- Willing to put in the work
- Wants a clear roadmap — not inspiration
- Slightly impatient — ready to go now

What makes them tune out:
- Vague motivation content — they're past that
- "Mindset" posts — they want tactics
- Too much theory — they want to build

What reaches them:
- Concrete step-by-step content
- Specific tools, specific actions, specific timelines
- Real examples of what's possible
- The blueprint — exactly what Tam is building

Content goal: convert follower to student/client.
They're ready — give them the next step.

Example angle:
"You have 2 hours a day and 6 months.
Here's exactly what I'd do with that."

## Funnel Content Mix (target per week)
- Level 1: 1 post — broad reach, awareness play
- Level 2: 1 post — reframe the dismissive majority
- Level 3: 2-3 posts — core audience, most content here
- Level 4: 1 post — convert the ready ones
