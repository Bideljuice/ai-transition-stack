# Content Routing Logic

## Content Type Detection

### Provocative Observation
Signals: challenges an assumption, invokes FOMO, 
short punchy idea, "most people don't know this"
→ Format: Single tweet OR short LinkedIn post
→ Length: Tweet max 280 chars, LinkedIn 100-200 words
→ Lead with: the cost of inaction or the missed opportunity

### Framework or Concept
Signals: explains a mental model, personal development idea,
"here's how to think about X", step-by-step thinking
→ Format: LinkedIn post with structure OR long-form article
→ Length: LinkedIn 200-400 words, Article 800-1500 words
→ Lead with: the counterintuitive insight, not the framework

### Transformational Story
Signals: personal experience, before/after, 
"this changed how I see X", tied to learning or money
→ Format: LinkedIn narrative post OR email newsletter
→ Length: LinkedIn 200-350 words, Email 300-500 words
→ Lead with: the moment everything changed, not the backstory

## Relevance Filter

### Publish Now
- Directly tied to AI, financial security, 
  job adaptation, or career relevance
- Challenges something Tam's audience believes right now
- Has a clear action or insight the reader can use today

### Reframe First
- Interesting but not immediately relevant to audience
- Needs connecting to AI/financial security angle
- Abstract concept that needs a concrete example

### Skip
- Pure entertainment with no insight
- Already widely covered — no new angle possible
- Not connected to adaptation, learning, or financial security

## Angle Detection

### FOMO angle
Reader is behind and doesn't know it yet
→ Lead with the gap they're missing
→ CTA: direct action they can take today

### Insight angle  
Reader thinks they know this but they're wrong
→ Lead with the reframe
→ CTA: reflection question — "what would you do differently?"

### Permission angle
Reader knows what to do but hasn't started
→ Lead with removing the excuse
→ CTA: "Ugly is fine. Start today."
